About

HackGT presents Catalyst, a FREE day of STEAM directed towards high school students in the Metro Atlanta Area. We are eager to welcome all of these students on Saturday, March 28th to Georgia Tech's Campus.

This will be an opportunity for participants to:

  • Learn about the world of STEAM and Computer Science as a career path.
  • Gain insight into impactful, widely known tech companies.
  • Receive mentorship from college students and industry professionals.
  • Connect with more opportunities at Georgia Tech & in the city to further their education and exposure.

During the Day

Throughout the day, students will follow one of four tracks: Hardware, Gaming, Information Security, or MusicTech.

Each track will have workshops and will allow students to build a piece of technology they can take home. In addition to the track workshops, there will be opportunities for students to engage with industry professionals and college students.

We want to show high school students the value of computer science. STEM is showing up in numerous subjects across the board, and at HackGT, we feel that every student should have access to the necessary materials to follow their dreams.

Tracks

Hardware

The hardware track will teach students the fundamentals of circuits through the interactions of the Arduino and the breadboard. Throughout the day, you will have lessons about the basics of electronics, slowly building on top of each other, and finishing the day by constructing your own robot. You will create and control your own devices and gain skills just enough to make you slightly dangerous. If you have a spark in interest, come join hardware!

Gaming

From Minecraft and Mario, to Roblox and Rocket League, video games are a popular and interactive way to code and launch experiences! The Gaming track focuses on teaching fundamental computing concepts by introducing game design with levels, animations, and characters using block-based coding. In this programming adventure, participants will level up by completing short challenges and activities to reinforce concepts learned and will finish the day with a fully created game in Scratch. Calling all future game designers and developers to create the next Fortnite!

Info Security

As you browse through Instagram, you're not the only one scrolling through your feed. Data is being collected every second, and you are no exception. Join us to learn more about protecting yourself and your devices through vulnerability protection, encryption, and cyber awareness!!

MusicTech

Are you interested in producing beats for the next biggest Soundcloud rapper? If so, this track is perfect for you! The music technology track focuses on the intersection between music and programming! This track will teach you how to code in either Python or Javascript to compose individual beats and ultimately, a song, through a program called EarSketch. Musicians aren't the only ones that can make catchy tunes. After going through this track, you, too, will be equipped with computer science skills to produce sick beats!

FAQ

Who can attend?

High school students from all school districts are welcome to register for the event!

Should I bring anything?

Nope! We will provide food for every participant and laptops for every team. Our sponsors will also have some free swag for you to take home!

Where is Catalyst?

The event will be in the Klaus Advanced Computing Building at Georgia Tech.

Are parents allowed to come?

Although we are grateful for parental support, this is a student-only event from check-in on.

How do I choose which track to attend?

Participants will be able to choose which track they would like to attend during registration.

What is HackGT?

HackGT is a student organization and 501(c)(3) non-profit at Georgia Tech devoted to Computer Science outreach in collegiate and high school communities. To learn more, check out our organization website here.
